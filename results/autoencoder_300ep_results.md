## Observations

- Val loss decreased monotonically across all 300 epochs; cosine LR schedule (1e-3 to 1e-5) created smooth convergence.
- KL divergence settled at ~10.6 (effective contribution to loss: 10.6 x 0.001 = 0.011, ~20% of total).
- No posterior collapse observed — latent space remained structured throughout training.
- AUROC of ~0.80 confirms the model learned meaningful separation between genuine and laundering transactions.
- Low AUPRC reflects the extreme class imbalance (0.14% positive rate) and the inherent limitation of reconstruction-based anomaly scoring — MSE across all features dilutes discriminative signal.
- Test fraud mean score (1.18) is significantly higher than val (0.096), suggesting different laundering patterns or outliers across splits.
